Disease Name: SINUSITIS
Overview: Sinusitis is inflammation or swelling of the tissue lining the sinuses. 
Symptoms: Common symptoms include nasal congestion, facial pain or pressure, headache, cough, fatigue, and a reduced sense of smell and taste.
Causes: Sinusitis can be caused by viral infections, bacterial infections, fungal infections, allergies, or structural issues such as nasal polyps or a deviated septum.
Risk Factors: Risk factors include a history of respiratory infections, allergies, asthma, nasal polyps, a weakened immune system, and environmental factors such as smoking or air pollution.
Complications: Complications of sinusitis may include chronic sinusitis, infections spreading to nearby structures, meningitis, and worsening of asthma symptoms.
Preventions: Preventive measures include avoiding known allergens, practicing good hygiene, using a humidifier, quitting smoking, and treating conditions such as allergies promptly.
Prescriptions/Medications: Treatment may involve over-the-counter or prescription medications such as decongestants, nasal corticosteroids, saline nasal sprays, pain relievers, antibiotics (if bacterial infection is present), and in severe cases, surgery.
Warning: Seek medical advice if symptoms persist or worsen, or if there are signs of a secondary infection or complications.
